Burbage nursery is rated outstanding by Ofsted

Little Explorers, which meets in the Scout hut in Britannia Road in the village, was visited in December 2014, and a glowing report on published this month (January). Inspector Catherine Sharkey, who awarded the pre-school the best of four possible gradings, said: “This provision is outstanding.” She said teaching was “very good”, leadership and management were “excellent” and that parents were “fully involved” in their children’s learning. Children were said to be “very happy and settled” and “extremely safe”. Staff are described as “kind and caring” and owner Alison Bates is praised for her “superb drive and dedication”. She said she was “thrilled” with the findings.

A daily routine gives children a sense of security. This is why we ensure each day is properly structured while leaving enough time for spontaneous activities.

The daily routine at a glance

Time Activity
08.45 am Arrival and self registration followed by free choice play. Some of us may go and do small group work. Observations are carried out. Focused Activities.
09.30 am Morning Prayer and snack bar operates around the session.
11.15 - 11.30 am 5 minute warning is given and then we all help to tidy messy play away.
11.30 - 11.45 am Circle time. A mixture of opportunities; we may join in our key person group, circle time, story time, dance and music.
11.45 - 12.30 pm Afternoon Prayer.
We wash our hands and then eat our lunches.
12.30 - 2.25 pm Free choice play, Adult-led time. Library book changes are made, Outdoor Play/ Crafts
2.25 - 2.30 pm 5 minutes warning and then we all help to tidy away.
2.30 - 2.40 pm Story
2.40 Shoes and Coat are put on, ready to go home.
